History

Overview

  • Culver City traces its roots back to the early 20th century when it was predominantly agricultural land. Harry Culver, the city's founder, began developing the area in 1913, envisioning it as a bustling metropolis. By 1917, Culver had successfully established Culver City as a distinct community, attracting residents and businesses with its promise of growth and opportunity. The city's initial years were marked by rapid expansion, driven by the emergence of the film industry, which found a home in Culver City due to its favorable climate and proximity to Los Angeles.

  • Hughes Aircraft Company Howard Hughes, the renowned aviator, filmmaker, and entrepreneur, left an indelible mark on Culver City's history. In the 1930s, Hughes purchased a significant portion of the RKO Studios lot, where he produced several acclaimed films. His innovative approach to filmmaking and aviation contributed to Culver City's reputation as a hub for creativity and innovation. Although his time in Culver City was relatively brief, Hughes' presence left a lasting legacy, shaping the city's cultural and industrial landscape.
  • The studios (1960s, 1970s and 1980s) The Hal Roach Studios met their end in 1963. Subsequently, in the late 1960s, owners sold much of the MGM backlot acreage (including lot 3 and other Jefferson Boulevard property) and the adjacent 28.5 acres known as RKO Forty Acres, previously owned by RKO Pictures and later Desilu Productions. By 1976, redevelopment saw the sets demolished. Presently, the RKO site forms the southern expansion of the Hayden Industrial Tract, while the MGM property has been transformed into a subdivision and a shopping center named Raintree Plaza.
